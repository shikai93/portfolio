WNDR

Project Overview

Wndr is a geo-based social media application developed by our team of three. The idea was conceptualized and tested by our clients and we were in charge of the software implementation.

Wndr allows users to post their current thoughts along with an emoji to depict their emotions in their current locations. Other users can then view thoughts nearby and either like or comment on them, generating a discussion thread.

This software was built on the steroid supersonic framework as an hybrid application using angular JS and HTML.

AGILE software project management techniques

Challenges

Our team had trouble adopting AGILE principles initially. We could not identify areas of improvement and were slow on adapting new working processes.

Over time, as we improved team communications and became more open to new ideas, we managed to adopt AGILE principles more effectively.

Trashifai

Project Overview

Trashifai was an idea conceptualized by my team during Wildhacks : 2016, a hackathon organized by Northwestern University that attracted more than 400 participants.

Trashifai is a smart waste sorting bin that detects and identifies waste being discarded into the bin. It would then determine if the waste is indeed a recyclable object and sorts it accordingly.

Trashifai was built on Clarifai, an image recognition software built on artificial intelligence, and Arduino for hardware interface.

Find out more about Trashifai here.


Lessons Learnt

Front End Programming

Programming in Angular JS, HTML and CSS.

Rapid Prototyping

Challenges

The 48 hour time limit was a challenge and forces us to ideate and prototype quickly. Initially, we could not brainstorm effectively to identify a potential problem that we want to solve. However, we managed to look back at our past experiences and eventually conceptualize Trashifai.

Trashifai was inspired by our experience in Germany, which takes recycling seriously. We often found it difficult to determine if an object is recyclable; for instance, although pizza boxes are made of cardboard, they cannot be recycled since they are soiled with grease and food items. Thus, we created a smart waste bin to help us identify the recyclables and sort them accordingly.

My role in the team was the creation of a mobile application that allows users to take a photo of the trash and identify if it is recyclable, hardware prototyping and the creation of our team website.

Illumination

Project Overview

Illumination is a capstone design project. Our team of 5 were tasked to design both a lighting support system and a lighting control system for our client's experiment setup.

The designed support system allows the light to translate across the horizontal plane above the experimental setup.

The electrical control system allows the light's brightness to be varied and automates the toggling of the light.


Lessons Learnt

Arduino Programming

Design Thinking and Prototyping

Challenges

The design of the support system requires mechanical engineering knowledge, which I had not learned before. Thus, I had to do multiple research before I can contribute effectively in design meetings. This project has also allowed me to learn about design thinking processes.

The circuitry and Arduino code design of the control system was done by me, and the lack of electronics knowledge made this a hard task. After multiple hours spent researching, I finally consulted the lab assistant for advice, and was able to build the circuitry successfully eventually. With the circuitry design complete, the coding of the Arduino was an esay task, since I had coding experience in Arduino.

All in all, I gained confidence in dealing with uncertainties and learnt about design thinking through this project.

AGILE software project management techniques

Challenges

The difference in work culture and language barrier made it hard for me to be integrated into the company successfully. However, the friendly environment in the company and weekly get-together organised where employees took turns to cook lunch for everyone were helpful in helping me bond with the team.

I managed to experience AGILE project management in a corporate environment. I realised that the key factor to ensure the effectiveness of AGILE project management was organization culture. Employers must be flexible and open to criticism, while employees must be willing to voice their own opinions for AGILE to be effective.

Thus, it is important to change the organization culture first before implementing any project management techniques. Techniques that suit a particular organization may not be effective in other organizations unless the work culture is changed.
